Frequently Asked Questions

Common troubleshooting topics and operational patterns for Veridot.

Why is my token being rejected?

If the signature is cryptographically valid but validation fails, check the following:

  • Liveness expiration: The session heartbeat has lapsed. Ensure your issuing service is renewing the active LIVENESS entry before the validUntil timestamp is reached.
  • Clock drift: If the clocks of your verifiers and signers differ by more than 5 minutes, KEY_EPOCH validation will fail. Ensure NTP is active on all instances.
  • Stale version: An out-of-order state update was rejected because its version was lower than the local watermark version.

Why are revocations slow to propagate?

If revocations take longer than 1 second to propagate to gateways, investigate the following:

  • Kafka topic partitions: If you are using Apache Kafka, check that topic partitions are balanced. Ensure your verifier nodes belong to unique consumer groups to avoid partition-lock delays.
  • SQL Indexing: For database-based brokers, make sure your index on the version column is active and optimized.

Is Veridot thread-safe?

Yes. The GenericSignerVerifier and broker adapters are thread-safe and designed to be used as shared singleton beans in multi-threaded environments (such as Spring Boot or Quarkus).

Can I verify tokens in mobile apps or browsers?

No, mobile applications and browsers must not perform local verification because they cannot securely verify capabilities or communicate directly with the database/broker. Instead, they should request token status from a public verification gateway microservice that runs the Veridot SDK.
